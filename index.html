Professional Profile

I am a final-year Physics student at the University of Barcelona (UB) with extensive hands-on experience in professional astronomy. I currently work as a support astronomer at the Sabadell Observatory (operated by the Sabadell Astronomical Society) while conducting research for my Bachelor's Thesis (TFG) at the Institute of Cosmos Sciences (ICCUB).

Highly skilled in the operation of professional-grade telescopes and instrumentation, photometric and spectroscopic data analysis and reduction, and Python programming for astronomical pipelines. For several years, I have been focused on observing and analyzing light curves of pulsating variable stars and exoplanet transits.

As a member of several amateur research groups at the Sabadell Astronomical Society (AAS), I am deeply integrated into the local amateur community, regularly attending conventions and actively participating in international Pro-Am collaborations such as ExoClock and GEOS.

Exoplanet Research

ExoClock & ESA Ariel Mission

Within the framework of the ExoClock collaboration, I regularly monitor exoplanet transits to support the ephemerides for the future ESA Ariel Mission. I specialize in detecting weak transits (< 2 mmag of depth) utilizing professional 1m-class telescopes, contributing to target selection and observation planning for the international network.
